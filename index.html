Mount Everest

Mount Everest, also known as Sagarmatha in Nepal and Chomolungma in Tibet, is the highest mountain in the world. It is located in the Mahalangur mountain range in the Himalayas, on the border between Nepal and China. The peak stands at an elevation of 8,848.86 meters (29,031.7 feet) above sea level.

Geography

Mount Everest is part of the Himalayan range and is situated in the Khumbu region of northeastern Nepal. It is located in Sagarmatha National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The mountain is renowned for its challenging climbing routes and harsh weather conditions.

Climbing

Mount Everest has been a popular destination for mountaineers from around the world since the early 20th century. The two main climbing routes are the southeast ridge from Nepal and the northeast ridge from Tibet. Climbing Mount Everest is a demanding and dangerous endeavor that requires careful preparation, physical fitness, and experience.

The base camp for Mount Everest expeditions is located at an altitude of approximately 5,364 meters (17,598 feet) above sea level. It serves as a starting point for climbers and provides essential facilities and support.

Trivia

  • Mount Everest was first successfully climbed by Sir Edmund Hillary and Tenzing Norgay in 1953.
  • More than 4,000 people have reached the summit of Mount Everest as of 2021.
  • The summit of Mount Everest is covered in snow and ice year-round.
